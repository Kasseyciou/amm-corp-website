TFDA Medical Device Approval

In Taiwan, TFDA medical device approval generally involves two main stages. The first stage is the Quality System Documentation (QSD) review, and the second stage is the product registration (license application) process.

Two Main Stages

QSD Review & Product Registration

01 QSD

Quality System Documentation

QSD Review

During the QSD stage, foreign manufacturers must undergo a quality system documentation assessment to demonstrate that their manufacturing and quality management systems comply with TFDA requirements and relevant international standards.

This includes preparing and submitting documents such as:

  • Quality manual
  • Process controls
  • Validation and verification reports
  • Risk management files
  • Change control procedures
  • Traceability and nonconforming product handling procedures
02 TFDA

License Application

Product Registration

After QSD approval, each medical device must go through TFDA product registration, also referred to as the “registration and market approval” stage. In this phase, TFDA reviews the safety and performance of the device, including technical documentation, clinical or literature evidence, risk analysis, labelling and IFU, test reports, and compliance with applicable standards.

The depth of review and documentation requirements depend on the device class (Class I, II or III) and its risk level.
